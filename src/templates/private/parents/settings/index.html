How to Design Your Reward Economy
Coin & point benchmarks for chores, store items, goals, and challenges.
Show guide
🏦 The two-currency system
🪙 10 coins = 💵 $1 — recommended real-money exchange rate
🪙 Coins — personal rewards

Belong to each kid individually. Spent in the Kid Store on personal rewards. Fosters individual responsibility.

⭐ Points — family teamwork

Accumulate across the whole family toward Family Goals. The whole family wins together.

🧹 Chore pricing benchmarks
Effort Examples 🪙 Coins ⭐ Pts
Quick (<5 min)Make bed, wipe counter5–105–10
Medium (10–20 min)Vacuum, sweep + mop15–2515–25
Hard (20–40 min)Clean bathroom, mow lawn30–5030–50
Big project (45+ min)Garage cleanout, car wash50–10050–100
🛍️ Kid store pricing
Small · 50–150 coins

Choose dinner, special snack, stay up 30 min late

Medium · 200–400 coins

Extra screen time, rent a movie, small toy

Big · 500–1000 coins

Special outing, new book, dollars store trip

🏆 Family goal sizing
Quick win · 200–400 pts

Movie night, pizza night · ~1 week (2 kids)

Medium · 600–1000 pts

Board game, bowling trip · 2–3 weeks

Big goal · 1500–3000 pts

Amusement park, camping trip · 1–2 months

⚡ Challenge difficulty
🥉 Bronze · 25–50 coins & pts

Single-day or simple behavior goal

🥈 Silver · 75–150 coins & pts

Multi-day or moderate difficulty

🥇 Gold · 200–400 coins & pts

High effort or multi-week commitment

✅ Quick tips
  • Start with 4–6 chores and add more once kids are engaged.
  • Calibrate after week one — raise/lower prices based on what you observe.
  • Never take coins away — keep the system trustworthy.
  • Always have a quick-win goal active alongside a bigger one.
  • Pause, don't delete when life gets busy.
